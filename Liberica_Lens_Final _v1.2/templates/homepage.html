What is Liberica Lens?

The website you're referring to hosts a comprehensive image dataset along with detailed morphological metadata. This metadata has been meticulously extracted using a sophisticated Trained UNet model, coupled with advanced morphological feature calculations. These processes have been employed to accurately determine the corresponding morphometric values. The significance of this public dataset is immense, particularly for future research studies focusing on Liberica Coffee Beans in Lipa City.

The dataset comprises images of Liberica Green Coffee beans that have been systematically collected from four distinct farms located in Lipa City. These farms are situated in various barangays, namely Brgy. Tipakan (Katy), Brgy. Talisay (Mukring), Brgy. San Benito (Saludo), and Brgy. Lodlod (Tunying). A rigorous data collection process was undertaken, where each farm contributed a total of 2000 images. This was achieved by capturing 1000 images showcasing the front view and another 1000 depicting the back view of the coffee beans.

The meticulous documentation of these images serves as a testament to the diversity and unique characteristics of the Liberica Coffee beans cultivated in these regions. Researchers and scholars can leverage this dataset to conduct in-depth analyses, draw comparisons, and potentially uncover novel insights into the morphological variations present across different cultivation practices and environmental conditions.

Furthermore, the dataset's accessibility to the public domain enhances its value, as it allows for widespread collaboration and knowledge sharing within the scientific community. This collaborative effort can pave the way for groundbreaking discoveries and contribute significantly to the agricultural and botanical study of Liberica Coffee beans, thereby enriching the collective understanding of this crop's cultivation and its implications on the coffee industry at large.
